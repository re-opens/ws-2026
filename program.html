Workshop Format and Program

RE-OpenS is designed as a half-day, paperless, and interactive workshop. It consists of three core blocks: a theoretical session, two practical sessions, and a feedback session. The workshop introduces participants to REFSQ’s Open Science Initiative and empowers them to apply open science best practices, infrastructures, services, and tools through hands-on exercises.

Session Descriptions

Theoretical Session: Introduces participants to the foundations of open science and REFSQ’s Open Science Initiative, including the Open Science Track, Policy, and Competition. Participants will learn about two key infrastructures—ORKG and ORKG Ask—that support FAIR scientific knowledge sharing.

Practical Session 1: Participants will use the SciKGTeX LaTeX package to annotate a publication with FAIR metadata. The exercise includes setting up an Overleaf project, embedding semantic annotations, and optionally uploading the result to ORKG.

Practical Session 2: Participants will explore ORKG Ask and the CSV import feature to create a structured comparison of scientific publications. The session includes querying, refining, exporting, and visualizing research data.

Feedback Session: Through a World Café format, participants will share insights and help shape the roadmap for future RE-OpenS editions. The session concludes with a reflection and closing remarks.
